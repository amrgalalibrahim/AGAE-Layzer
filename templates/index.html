About Lysine Lactylation

What is Lactylation?

Lysine lactylation is a novel post-translational modification (PTM) where lactate-derived lactyl groups are added to lysine residues. This epigenetic mark plays crucial roles in gene regulation, cellular metabolism, and disease pathogenesis, particularly in cancer and inflammation.

Why Predict Lactylation Sites?

Identifying lactylation sites helps researchers understand protein function regulation, metabolic reprogramming, and disease mechanisms. Computational prediction accelerates discovery by prioritizing experimental validation targets and revealing novel regulatory mechanisms.

How the Analysis Works

1

Input Processing

Automatically detects sequence type (Protein/DNA/RNA). For nucleic acids, performs 6-frame translation to generate all possible protein sequences.

2

Feature Extraction

Identifies all lysine (K) residues and extracts flanking sequence windows. Computes physicochemical properties and sequence composition features.

3

ML Prediction

Applies selected machine learning model (AGEA, LSTM, CNN, etc.) to predict lactylation probability with confidence scores for each site.
